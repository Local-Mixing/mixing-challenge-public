Our first challenge concerns random reversible circuits. Gowers conjectured that random $$n$$-wire, $$m$$-gate reversible circuits compute pseudorandom functions. If the conjecture holds, then we should be able to use random reversible circuits as a block cipher -- where the key is the description of the circuit $$C$$. Then, $$\mathsf{Enc}(C, x) = C(x)$$ and $$\mathsf{Dec}(C, y)=C^{-1}(y)$$.
